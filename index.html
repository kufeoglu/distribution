DSO, DNO, DISCOM, IOU — Terminology & Duties Around the World

WHY THIS MATTERS. "DSO" (Distribution System Operator) is a European Union legal term defined under the Electricity Directives — the operator of the medium- and low-voltage network that delivers power from the transmission grid to end customers. The rest of the world uses different names for the same job — and the bundle of duties varies sharply. Some "DSOs" only operate the wires; others own them, bill customers, set retail tariffs, run flexibility markets, and procure power. Counting "DSOs" alone hides this.

Unlike at transmission level, distribution operators almost always own the assets they operate (local-monopoly economics). The interesting splits at the distribution layer are: (1) wires-only vs. wires+retail bundled; (2) ownership type (state, municipal, investor, cooperative); (3) the emerging DNO→DSO transformation as networks become "smart" with EVs, rooftop solar, batteries, and demand response.

DSO Distribution System Operator (EU) — operates and usually owns the MV/LV network. Now also expected to procure local flexibility services.
DNO Distribution Network Operator (UK) — historic term: owns and operates the wires only. Currently transitioning into a wider "DSO" role under Ofgem RIIO-ED2 (2023–28).
IDNO Independent Distribution Network Operator (UK) — smaller licensed operators serving new build sites (housing developments, business parks). ~10 main groups in GB.
DISCOM Distribution Company (India) — ~70 state-government-owned utilities; do wires + retail + tariff setting. Heavy AT&C losses in many states.
DISCO Distribution Company (MENA / South Asia / Africa) — generic acronym used in Egypt (8), Iraq (18), Iran (39), Pakistan (11), Nigeria (11), Türkiye (21).
IOU Investor-Owned Utility (USA) — privately owned, regulated by state PUCs. ~170 IOUs; serve ~70% of US customers (PG&E, Duke, ConEd, etc.).
POU / Municipal Publicly Owned Utility — community-owned (city/county). USA: ~2,000 (LADWP, SMUD, NYPA). EU: Stadtwerke (Germany ~800).
Co-op Rural Electric Cooperative — member-owned, mainly rural. USA: ~900 (NRECA), 56% of US land area. Bangladesh: ~80 PBSs.
DNSP Distribution Network Service Provider (Australia) — 13 in NEM + Western Power in WEM. Mix of state-owned and private.
LDC Local Distribution Company (Canada) — ~60 in Ontario alone (Hydro One Networks largest), provincially regulated.
Concessionária Distribution Concessionaire (Brazil) — 53 concessions awarded by ANEEL; mix of private (Enel, EDP, Equatorial) and state.
Stadtwerke Municipal utility (Germany) — ~800+ local utilities, often vertically bundling distribution + retail + heat + water + sometimes generation.
Worked example: Great Britain — 14 DNO licences, 6 corporate groups, 1 nation in transition

Ofgem licences 14 Distribution Network Operator areas in Great Britain — but they're owned by just six corporate groups. Each group's licences are operated as a single business with shared boards, control rooms, and price controls (RIIO-ED2 covers 2023–2028). Ofgem deliberately keeps the licence count high to allow benchmarking ("if SSEN can hit this target, why can't Northern Powergrid?"), while the corporate consolidation reflects scale economies. Northern Ireland has its own separate DNO (NIE Networks, owned by Irish state utility ESB). Add ~10 main IDNOs (Independent DNOs) serving new-build housing and commercial sites, and a wider DNO→DSO transformation programme that is fundamentally changing what these companies do.
